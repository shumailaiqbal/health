Diabetes Management Guide

A comprehensive lifestyle, dietary, and workout plan designed to stabilize blood sugar levels and promote long-term metabolic health.

Condition Overview

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic condition characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (or blood sugar). It occurs when the body either doesn't produce enough insulin or can't effectively use the insulin it makes.

Through careful dietary management focusing on low-glycemic foods, paired with consistent physical activity, many individuals can significantly improve their blood sugar control and mitigate associated risks.

Dietary Chart

Focus on nutrient-dense, fiber-rich foods while minimizing processed carbs and hidden sugars.

Foods to Eat
  • ✓ Non-starchy vegetables (Spinach, Broccoli, Kale)
  • ✓ Whole grains (Quinoa, Brown Rice, Oats)
  • ✓ Lean proteins (Chicken breast, Fish, Tofu)
  • ✓ Healthy fats (Avocados, Nuts, Olive Oil)
  • ✓ Legumes (Lentils, Chickpeas)
Foods to Avoid
  • ✗ Sugary beverages (Sodas, Sweetened Teas)
  • ✗ Refined carbohydrates (White Bread, Pastries)
  • ✗ Highly processed snacks & fast food
  • ✗ Sweetened yogurts and cereals
  • ✗ Excessive alcohol consumption

Daily Meal Plan Suggestion

Breakfast (8:00 AM)

Oatmeal and Berries: 1/2 cup of rolled oats cooked in almond milk, topped with a handful of fresh blueberries, walnuts, and a pinch of cinnamon.

Lunch (1:00 PM)

Grilled Chicken Salad: Mixed greens topped with grilled chicken breast, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, avocado, and a light olive oil & lemon dressing.

Dinner (7:00 PM)

Baked Salmon with Greens: 4oz baked wild salmon prepared with lemon and herbs, served alongside steamed asparagus and 1/2 cup of quinoa.

Snack Options (Optional)

A small handful of almonds, celery sticks with hummus, or 1/2 of an apple with natural peanut butter.

Exercise Recommendations

Cardiovascular exercise increases cellular sensitivity to insulin, helping your body utilize blood sugar more effectively. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week.

Recommended Workouts:

  • Brisk walking for 30 minutes daily
  • Low-impact cycling or swimming
  • Light resistance training 2x per week
